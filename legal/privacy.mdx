# Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2025

Your privacy is important to us. It is our policy to respect your privacy and comply with any applicable law and regulation regarding any personal information we may collect about you, including across our website, https://brewit.money, and other sites we own and operate as well as mobile applications we offer. Wherever possible, we have designed our website so that you may navigate and use our website without having to provide Personal Data.

This Privacy Policy describes how we, as a controller, collect, use and share your personal data. It applies to personal data you voluntarily provide to us, or that is automatically collected by us.

In this policy, "**we**", "**us**" and "**our**" refers to Consenso Labs Private Limited., Bangalore, India. Any data protection related questions you might have about how we handle your personal data or if you wish to exercise your data subject rights, please contact us by post or at **admin@consensolabs.com**.

In this Policy, "**personal data**" means any information relating to you as an identified or identifiable natural person (“Data Subject”); an identifiable natural person is one who can be identified, directly or indirectly, in particular by reference to an identifier such as a name, an online identifier or to one or more factors specific to your physical, physiological, genetic, mental, economic, cultural or social identity.

In this Policy, "**processing**" means any operation or set of operations which is performed on personal data or on sets of personal data, whether or not by automated means, such as collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure or destruction.

### 1. Glossary

- **Blockchain**: A decentralized digital ledger for recording transactions across multiple computers.
- **Transaction**: A record of an interaction or operation on the blockchain.
- **Smart Contract**: A self-executing contract with the terms of the agreement directly written into code.
- **Token**: A digital asset issued on a blockchain.
- **Wallet**: A digital tool enabling blockchain interactions and token management.
- **Public Key**: A cryptographic code enabling others to send data to a wallet.
- **Private Key**: A confidential code that provides control over the wallet.
- **Account**: A user identity managed through the Brewit platform that interacts with blockchain services.
- **Brewit Interface(s)**: The web and mobile interfaces provided by Brewit for user interaction with blockchain technologies and features.

---

### 2. Your Information and the Blockchain

When you use Brewit to interact with the blockchain, your public wallet address and transaction history become publicly visible. The blockchain is immutable and globally accessible, which means data recorded cannot be deleted or altered. We do not control the data published on-chain nor can we delete it.

### 3. How We Use Personal Data

We use your data for:

- **One-click Onboarding**: We may process your wallet address and usage data to personalize your experience.
- **Swift Transactions**: We track transaction status and performance metrics to improve reliability.
- **Built-In Security**: IP addresses, login timestamps, and request metadata are processed for fraud prevention.
- **Accounts Management**: We use identifiers and cookies to maintain sessions and secure account activities.

**Third-party service providers include:**

- **Alchemy**: RPC and webhook data  
- **Zerion**: NFT and transaction data  
- **Metamask**: Price APIs  
- **Dynamic**: Signing facilitation  
- **Pimlico**: Bundler and paymaster operations  
- **Matcha 0x**: Swap aggregation  
- **Vercel**: Hosting and API delivery  
- **Cloudflare**: DNS and performance security  
- **AWS**: Log and database storage via S3, EC2, CloudFront  

Legal bases for processing include consent, performance of a contract, legal obligation, and legitimate interest.

### 9. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under UK GDPR, you have rights to:

- Access and obtain a copy of your data  
- Rectify inaccurate or incomplete data  
- Request erasure (excluding blockchain data)  
- Restrict or object to processing  
- Data portability  
- Not be subject to automated decision-making  
- Object to direct marketing  
- Withdraw consent  
- Lodge a complaint with the ICO  

To exercise your rights, contact us at **admin@consensolabs.com**.
